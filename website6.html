PARAGRAPH TAG

The paragraph tag is used to... well write a paragraph. You can use as many as you want and can style them in css. A paragraph will always start on a new line, ignoring spaces or new lines and It's defined by writing p between lower than and higher than symbols. Here's an example:

THE IMAGE TAG

This is the tag used to put cat images into your magnificent cat vlog. Its defined by the img tag and it makes use of a couple of attributes

These atributtes consist of:

"src" - In which you type the name and extension of the source file, basically the image itself.

"alt" - Indicates the alternative text.

"width" - Sets the width of the image (in pixels).

"height" - You guessed it, sets the height of the image (yes, pixels).

"alt"- Represents alternative text that will be displayed if the image in question cannot be found.

FORMATTING TAGS

This is a collection of tags that are used in order to alter the text. They can be placed inside parragraph tags to select which part of the text you want to format.

b - Used for bold text: Look at me im booooold

i - For italic text (remeber that tower in Italy?): Buongiorno raggazzi

strong - Behaves just like bold text, but remarks that the text is very important: This text is EXTREMELY important!

em - Which emphasizes text and similar to the strong tag, its identical to his twin - the italic tag. Only difference is that the emphasis tag will make screen readers read aloud the defined text differently: Use a screen reader, I dare you.

small - Pretty intuitive, makes the text be displayed way smaller: Im small and cute

mark - Defines text that should be highlighted, similar to markers in notebooks: Today is a great day.

del - It slashes the indicated text with a horizontal line: The colour of the sky is yellow blue.

ins - Stands for inserted, it underlines text: An apple a day keeps the doctor away!

sup - No, its not a greeting, its short for superscript. Superscripted text will be displayed half a character above the usual starting point for lines, and most of the times is displayed in a smaller font: This text is bananas.

sub - Similar to its counterpart, only difference is that (you guessed it) the text will be displayed below the usual starting point: Down there! There's a text here.


QUOTATION TAGS

This selection of tags is used in order to cite other websites, mark abbreviations or just quote... well... quotes.


q - This small basic tag is the one used to quote: Quoth the raven... nevermore

blockquote - Quote tag's bigger brother. Blockquoted text will be placed in a new line. This tag is used to specify that the information has been taken from another source. Ill leave some info from one of my favourite bands - cited from wikipedia

Slipknot is an American heavy metal band formed in Des Moines, Iowa, in 1995 by percussionist Shawn Crahan, drummer Joey Jordison and bassist Paul Gray.

abbr - Ever wondered what a weird bunch of letters means? This tag is the solution. Mark abbreviations with this tag to specify their full meaning. Hovering the cursor over the acronym will reveal what it stands for: Ever wondered what HTML stands for?

adress - Defines the contact information of the author. The text will be diplayed in italic and a line break will be added automatically.
Written by Jon Doe
Africa, Sahara desert

cite - Remarks the title of a creative work - usually displayed in italics:

Starry Night Painting by Vincent Van Gogh

bbdo - Stands for bidirectional override, and it can change the direction of the text to left to right: Even freakier text lololo
